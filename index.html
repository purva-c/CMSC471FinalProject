107 Years of
European-Only Inspectors.

Michelin launched in France in 1900. Japan: 2007. South Korea: 2016. Southeast Asia: 2017–18. Decades of European-only inspectors set the definition of "fine dining" before the rest of the world was ever evaluated.

Asia is the exception →

Cheap Stars
Only Exist in Asia.

Highlight Asian restaurants. They're the only place where 1-star and 2-star restaurants appear at the cheapest price tiers. Singapore hawker stalls. Tokyo ramen counters. A $10 bowl can earn a star. A $10 croque-monsieur in Paris cannot.

04 — The Invisible World

Where Michelin
Has Never Looked

Michelin covers ~45 of the world's 195 countries. The regions below have little to zero presence — not because they lack exceptional food, but because no inspectors have been sent.

RegionCoverage noteListings

Ethiopia has 80+ distinct regional cuisines. West Africa's jollof traditions stretch across a dozen nations. Mexico's 31 states rival Europe's culinary diversity. The guide doesn't call these places "not excellent" — it simply hasn't decided to look yet.

The Map Is Not the Territory.
The Guide Is Not Gastronomy.

The Michelin Guide is a remarkable dataset — but what it measures most clearly is the history of a French company's expansion decisions. The restaurants it lists are genuinely extraordinary. The restaurants it has never visited may be too.
